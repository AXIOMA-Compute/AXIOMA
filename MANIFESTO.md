## 1. Purpose

AXIOMA/Compute establishes a computational foundation where correctness, execution, and decision-making are governed by mathematics rather than trust, authority, or unverifiable processes.

The objective is clear:

Replace trust with proof.  
Replace opinion with determinism.  
Replace manipulation with mathematical integrity.

---

## 2. Core Principles

### 2.1 Mathematical Truth Over Interpretation  
AXIOMA enforces correctness through immutable constraints. Human bias or political influence cannot change truth.

### 2.2 Verifiable External Computation  
Any entity may compute, but only results proven correct may modify state. This ensures permissionlessness without sacrificing integrity.

### 2.3 Deterministic State Transitions  
All state changes must be provably correct and fully reproducible.

### 2.4 Transparency Without Exposure  
AXIOMA allows verifiable computation without revealing underlying data. Privacy and correctness coexist.

### 2.5 No Trusted Parties  
AXIOMA does not rely on trusted institutions, authorities, validators, corporations, or individuals.  
It relies solely on mathematics.

---

## 3. Structural Opposition

AXIOMA stands against:

- unverifiable decisions  
- opaque governance  
- black-box AI behavior  
- falsified research  
- economic data manipulation  
- unverifiable computation  
- structural corruption

## 4. Long-Term Vision

A world where:

- AI outputs are deterministic and provable  
- scientific work is reproducible

- digital truth cannot be forged  

AXIOMA aims to form a foundational layer for verifiable global systems.
